What is Scratch?

Scratch is a fun, free, and beginner-friendly programming language for everyone!

Scratch is a visual programming language developed by the Lifelong Kindergarten Group at the MIT Media Lab. It makes it easy for anyone—especially young people aged 8 to 16—to create their own interactive stories, games, and animations. Instead of typing complex code, you snap together colorful programming blocks to build your projects.

With Scratch, you can:

  • Design and share your own interactive games and animations.
  • Learn fundamental concepts of coding, computational thinking, and logic.
  • Express your creativity and tell your stories in new ways.
  • Join a vibrant global community to share, remix, and collaborate on projects.

Scratch is widely used in schools, coding clubs, and homes all over the world. It empowers students to develop crucial 21st-century skills like problem-solving, creativity, systematic reasoning, and collaborative work.
